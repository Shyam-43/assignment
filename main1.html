Rules of Cricket

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard pitch.

Rules include runs, wickets, overs, and various ways to get out! Runs are scored by hitting the ball and running between wickets or hitting boundaries. A wicket consists of three stumps topped by two bails. Various ways exist for a player to get out, such as being bowled, caught, run out, stumped, or leg before wicket (LBW).

The game comprises overs, each consisting of a set number of deliveries (usually six balls per over).

Cricket Teams

Explore the world's famous cricket teams and their achievements.

Teams like India, Australia, England, Pakistan, South Africa, West Indies, and others have a significant impact in international cricket. These teams participate in tournaments like the ICC Cricket World Cup, ICC Champions Trophy, and bilateral series, showcasing their skills and strategies.

History of Cricket

The history of cricket dates back several centuries. Originating in England, the sport evolved from games played during the medieval period.

It gained popularity in the 18th century and gradually spread across the British Empire and beyond. Over time, it transformed into a professional sport, witnessing changes in rules, equipment, and playing styles.

From the invention of Test cricket to the emergence of limited-overs formats like One Day Internationals (ODIs) and Twenty20 (T20), cricket's history is rich and diverse.
